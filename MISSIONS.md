## ACT 1 — FIRST DESCENT
### Missions 1–4

---

### M01 — Sea Trials
**Tagline:** Every legend begins with a systems check.

**Narrative:**
The HMS Leviathan, a state-of-the-art research submarine, has just completed construction under a joint civilian-scientific program. Your crew — newly assembled and untested — has been assigned to conduct its first live sea trial off the Atlantic continental shelf. What should be routine quickly becomes something else. As systems come online, Sonar detects an anomaly: a faint, repeating signal far below known mapping zones. It doesn't match any known marine life, geological activity, or human transmission. Command dismisses it as interference — but the signal repeats… with rhythm.

**Science Concept:** Basic sonar physics — active vs passive sonar, sound wave reflection, signal interpretation.

**Critical Station:** Sonar

**Moral Decision:** Follow protocol and ignore the anomaly — or investigate something unexplained without authorization.

**Station Actions:**
| Station | Action |
|---|---|
| Captain | Chooses "Investigate Signal" vs "Continue Trial" — final confirm button |
| Navigator | Adjusts heading slider to align with faint signal bearing |
| Sonar | Tunes frequency dial to isolate anomaly from background noise |
| Engineer | Balances power between propulsion and sonar clarity (power allocation bars) |
| Weapons | Deploys calibration ping — timed button press to send clean signal |

**Secret Discovered:** The signal is not random — it follows a structured interval, like a coded pulse.

**Cliffhanger:** As the mission ends, the signal abruptly stops… and something echoes back — as if it heard you.

---

### M02 — The Silent Echo
**Tagline:** Something down there is listening.

**Narrative:**
Authorized for a limited investigation, the Leviathan descends beyond standard operational depth. The pressure creaks against the hull as the crew enters the twilight zone — where sunlight disappears and sonar becomes your only vision. The signal returns — stronger now — but distorted. Sonar reveals impossible reflections: shapes that shouldn't exist at this depth. Navigator charts a trench that doesn't appear on official maps.

**Science Concept:** Ocean depth zones + bathymetry — mapping the seafloor using sonar data.

**Critical Station:** Navigator

**Moral Decision:** Trust official maps and stay safe — or rely on your own data and enter unknown territory.

**Station Actions:**
| Station | Action |
|---|---|
| Captain | Approves descent into uncharted zone (risk meter decision) |
| Navigator | Manually plots course through unmapped trench using waypoint placement |
| Sonar | Interprets distorted returns — pattern-matching shapes from signal noise |
| Engineer | Reinforces hull — activates pressure dampening systems in sequence |
| Weapons | Fires directional sonar bursts to map blind zones (angle selection + timing) |

**Secret Discovered:** The trench appears artificially carved — too smooth, too precise to be natural.

**Cliffhanger:** A sudden pressure wave hits the sub… but no seismic event is recorded.

---

### M03 — Below the Map
**Tagline:** Some places were never meant to be found.

**Narrative:**
Command denies the trench's existence. Official charts show nothing. Yet your instruments — and your eyes — say otherwise. The crew pushes deeper, navigating tight walls that seem engineered rather than formed. Strange metallic fragments appear along the trench floor — corroded, ancient… but not geological. Engineer detects trace radiation levels — faint, but unmistakable.

**Science Concept:** Radiation detection underwater + material corrosion under high pressure.

**Critical Station:** Engineer

**Moral Decision:** Report findings and risk mission shutdown — or conceal evidence to continue the discovery.

**Station Actions:**
| Station | Action |
|---|---|
| Captain | Decides whether to log or suppress anomaly report |
| Navigator | Slows sub and holds position over debris field (precision positioning control) |
| Sonar | Scans fragments and flags non-natural shapes |
| Engineer | Runs radiation scan and isolates affected systems (toggle system shutdowns) |
| Weapons | Launches micro-probe to retrieve fragment (trajectory mini-control) |

**Secret Discovered:** A fragment bears markings resembling Cold War-era military insignia… from no known nation.

**Cliffhanger:** The signal spikes — now clearly directional — leading further down into total darkness.

---

### M04 — First Contact Depth
**Tagline:** The deeper you go, the less alone you are.

**Narrative:**
The Leviathan reaches depths beyond any recorded human dive. Systems strain under pressure. Sonar begins returning delayed echoes — as if sound itself is behaving differently. Then — contact. A massive structure appears ahead, partially buried in sediment. Not rock. Not wreckage. Something constructed… and impossibly old.

**Science Concept:** Deep-sea pressure limits + hull integrity — compression forces at extreme depth.

**Critical Station:** Captain

**Moral Decision:** Push beyond safe limits for discovery — or protect the crew and retreat.

**Station Actions:**
| Station | Action |
|---|---|
| Captain | Calls "Advance" or "Abort" under rising pressure |
| Navigator | Maintains exact distance from structure (fine thrust adjustments) |
| Sonar | Switches between passive/active modes to track structure edges |
| Engineer | Manages hull stress — real-time pressure gauge balancing |
| Weapons | Charges defensive countermeasures — hold-to-charge system in case of impact |

**Secret Discovered:** The signal originates from inside the structure — and it changes when you get closer.

**Cliffhanger:** Lights flicker. Systems reboot. When they return… the structure is closer than before.

## ACT 2 — THE DEEPENING
### Missions 5–9

---

### M05 — The Leviathan Protocol
**Tagline:** Someone knew this was here.

**Narrative:**
Command abruptly intervenes. A classified directive — "Leviathan Protocol" — overrides your mission. You are ordered to withdraw immediately. No explanation given. But before retreating, Engineer intercepts encrypted data hidden within the sub's own systems — files that predate your mission… and reference the trench.

**Science Concept:** Submarine command hierarchy + encrypted military data systems.

**Critical Station:** Captain

**Moral Decision:** Obey direct orders — or challenge authority to uncover what Command is hiding.

**Station Actions:**
| Station | Action |
|---|---|
| Captain | Accepts or overrides command recall order |
| Navigator | Plots exit route vs hidden coordinates (branching map selection) |
| Sonar | Detects encrypted signal embedded in system noise |
| Engineer | Decrypts hidden files (pattern alignment puzzle) |
| Weapons | Locks or unlocks external comms array (security override switch) |

**Secret Discovered:** The submarine itself was designed specifically for this trench.

**Cliffhanger:** The signal shifts — now resembling a countdown.

---

### M06 — Ghosts of the Cold Ocean
**Tagline:** The past isn't gone. It's waiting.

**Narrative:**
Following hidden coordinates from the intercepted data, the crew discovers a wreck — an advanced submarine, decades old, resting impossibly deep. Inside: logs from a secret Cold War experiment known as Project Abyssal Reach — an attempt to harness deep-sea energy signals.

**Science Concept:** Acoustic communication + how sound travels farther in deep cold water (SOFAR channel).

**Critical Station:** Sonar

**Moral Decision:** Treat the past as a warning and stop — or continue despite knowing others failed before you.

**Station Actions:**
| Station | Action |
|---|---|
| Captain | Orders investigation of wreck vs bypass |
| Navigator | Positions sub alongside unstable wreck without collision |
| Sonar | Reconstructs distorted audio logs (waveform alignment interaction) |
| Engineer | Restores partial power to wreck systems (circuit routing puzzle) |
| Weapons | Cuts open access point using precision tool (timed heat meter control) |

**Secret Discovered:** The logs describe "contact" with something that responded intelligently to sonar.

**Cliffhanger:** A distorted voice recording plays: *"It's not a signal… it's a response."*

---

### M07 — Pressure Point
**Tagline:** The ocean is pushing back.

**Narrative:**
As the Leviathan descends again, environmental conditions become unstable. Pressure fluctuations occur in waves — unnatural and rhythmic. Sonar detects moving shapes outside the sub — too large, too fast, and silent between pulses.

**Science Concept:** Resonance and harmonic frequencies — how vibrations can amplify and damage structures.

**Critical Station:** Engineer

**Moral Decision:** Shut down systems to stay safe — or stay operational to understand the phenomenon.

**Station Actions:**
| Station | Action |
|---|---|
| Captain | Orders full shutdown vs maintain operations |
| Navigator | Stabilizes sub against shifting currents (counter-thrust balancing) |
| Sonar | Tracks moving external objects between signal pulses |
| Engineer | Dampens resonance by tuning system frequencies (match target waveform) |
| Weapons | Deploys decoy pulses to redirect external movement (timed pulse release) |

**Secret Discovered:** The signal appears to manipulate pressure and sound waves.

**Cliffhanger:** The hull groans — not from pressure, but from resonance… like something is tuning the sub.

---

### M08 — The Deep Architects
**Tagline:** We were not the first to explore the deep.

**Narrative:**
The structure is revisited — this time with clearer scans. It reveals patterns: corridors, chambers, geometry too precise for natural formation. Inside, the crew finds evidence of a long-lost civilization — one that adapted to extreme pressure environments.

**Science Concept:** Sound as a physical force — acoustic manipulation, resonance shaping matter (grounded in real principles, extended fictionally).

**Critical Station:** Sonar

**Moral Decision:** Study and potentially use the technology — or leave it untouched out of respect and fear.

**Station Actions:**
| Station | Action |
|---|---|
| Captain | Authorizes entry into structure |
| Navigator | Guides sub through tight interior corridors (slow path tracing) |
| Sonar | Activates chamber by emitting correct frequency sequence |
| Engineer | Powers ancient systems safely (voltage regulation sliders) |
| Weapons | Emits controlled resonance bursts to stabilize structure (precision timing) |

**Secret Discovered:** The civilization used sonar as both communication and construction — shaping matter with sound.

**Cliffhanger:** A chamber activates — responding directly to your sonar ping.

---

### M09 — Signal War
**Tagline:** Not everything wants to be found.

**Narrative:**
Another vessel appears — a modern military submarine, sent to secure or destroy the site. They attempt to jam your systems and seize control of the signal. The deep becomes a battlefield — not of weapons, but of waves, frequencies, and control over the unknown.

**Science Concept:** Electronic warfare + sonar jamming and frequency interference.

**Critical Station:** Weapons (signal warfare / countermeasures)

**Moral Decision:** Cooperate with rivals, compete for control, or protect the signal from everyone.

**Station Actions:**
| Station | Action |
|---|---|
| Captain | Chooses ally / compete / isolate strategy |
| Navigator | Maneuvers to avoid collision with rival sub |
| Sonar | Identifies enemy jamming frequencies |
| Engineer | Shields systems from interference (frequency filtering toggles) |
| Weapons | Actively jams or counters enemy signal (real-time frequency matching battle) |

**Secret Discovered:** The signal is a warning — not a beacon.

**Cliffhanger:** The countdown reaches zero… and the trench begins to open.

## ACT 3 — THE ABYSS AWAKENS
### Missions 10–12

---

### M10 — The Opening
**Tagline:** The ocean has a door. And it just unlocked.

**Narrative:**
The trench fractures, revealing a vast abyss beneath — deeper than any known point on Earth. Strange energy pulses upward, disrupting currents and electronics globally. The structure begins transmitting a new signal — far more powerful than before.

**Science Concept:** Ocean trench geology + tectonic movement and pressure release.

**Critical Station:** Navigator

**Moral Decision:** Descend into greater danger — or pull back and warn the surface world.

**Station Actions:**
| Station | Action |
|---|---|
| Captain | Decides to descend into newly opened abyss |
| Navigator | Plots descent path through collapsing terrain |
| Sonar | Maps expanding void in real time |
| Engineer | Maintains structural integrity under shifting pressure (dynamic load balancing) |
| Weapons | Clears falling debris using targeted pulse bursts (aim + timing) |

**Secret Discovered:** The original civilization didn't disappear — they sealed something below.

**Cliffhanger:** Something rises — massive, unseen, and moving toward the surface.

---

### M11 — The Last Transmission
**Tagline:** To stop it, you must understand it.

**Narrative:**
The crew accesses the final records of the ancient builders. They describe a force — a living resonance — capable of reshaping oceans through sound. They didn't create it. They contained it.

**Science Concept:** Signal generation + energy systems — how submarines produce and sustain power and signals.

**Critical Station:** Engineer

**Moral Decision:** Attempt to communicate with or control the entity — or avoid interaction and contain it indirectly.

**Station Actions:**
| Station | Action |
|---|---|
| Captain | Commits to attempt communication vs containment |
| Navigator | Holds exact position within signal origin point |
| Sonar | Translates incoming alien waveform into playable pattern |
| Engineer | Builds output signal using system-wide energy routing |
| Weapons | Amplifies and broadcasts final signal (timed multi-stage activation) |

**Secret Discovered:** The Leviathan's systems were designed to replicate the containment signal.

**Cliffhanger:** You realize — stopping it may require shutting down your own sub… permanently.

---

### M12 — Leviathan Rising
**Tagline:** Some missions change the world. Others save it.

**Narrative:**
The entity ascends, destabilizing oceans worldwide. Storm systems intensify. Coastlines are threatened. Time is running out. Only the Leviathan can recreate the signal strong enough to contain it — but doing so will overload the submarine.

**Science Concept:** Systems integration + coordinated signal output — multi-system synchronization under load.

**Critical Station:** Captain (with full crew coordination)

**Moral Decision:** Sacrifice the Leviathan to save the surface world — or preserve the crew and risk global consequences.

**Station Actions:**
| Station | Action |
|---|---|
| Captain | Initiates final sequence: sacrifice vs retreat |
| Navigator | Locks sub into precise alignment for maximum signal projection |
| Sonar | Maintains stable frequency lock against overwhelming interference |
| Engineer | Pushes reactor beyond safe limits (heat vs output balancing) |
| Weapons | Synchronizes final signal burst with entire crew (perfect-timing group trigger) |

**Secret Discovered:** The entity responds not just to sound — but to coordinated human input. Teamwork itself is the key.

**Final Hook:** As the signal stabilizes and the threat subsides… a faint new pulse emerges — from somewhere even deeper.

*The story isn't over.*
